Developer Handover Cleaning Guide

When a construction or refurbishment project reaches completion, properties must be presented in an inspection-ready condition before being handed to owners, tenants or property managers. Developer handover cleaning ensures that all remaining construction residue, dust and installation marks are removed prior to final sign-off.

This stage is often referred to as a post-construction or after builders clean and forms the final preparation step before occupation.

Why Handover Cleaning Is Required

Even when builders remove major debris, construction work leaves fine dust and installation residue throughout the property. Without a structured cleaning process these materials continue settling onto surfaces and can affect presentation during property inspection.

  • Fine plaster and sanding dust
  • Cement residue and grout haze
  • Silicone and adhesive marks
  • Protective film residue on new fittings
  • Dust inside frames, fixtures and ventilation areas

Typical Developer Handover Cleaning Stages

Stage 1 – Debris and Surface Reset

  • Removal of protective coverings and packaging
  • Collection of minor construction debris
  • Initial dust control and ventilation

Stage 2 – Full Dust Extraction

  • High-level dust removal from ceilings and fittings
  • Frame, fixture and joinery detailing
  • Vacuum extraction of fine plaster dust

Stage 3 – Surface Cleaning

  • Kitchen and bathroom fixture cleaning
  • Internal glazing and frame cleaning
  • Switches, sockets and skirting detailing

Stage 4 – Final Presentation Finish

  • Floor vacuum and damp mop where appropriate
  • Glass polishing
  • Final inspection-ready wipe down

Properties That Typically Require Handover Cleaning

  • New build homes
  • Property refurbishments
  • Apartment developments
  • Commercial-to-residential conversions
  • Large renovation projects

Common Issues Identified During Handover Cleaning

  • Dust inside newly installed cupboards and units
  • Protective film left on appliances or glass
  • Silicone residue around fixtures
  • Fine plaster dust embedded in flooring
  • Construction dust inside extractor vents

A structured after builders cleaning process ensures these issues are resolved before final property presentation.
